Czech Republic Visa

Are you an Indian passport holder looking to travel to the Czech Republic? Then you have come to the right place. The Czech Republic is an incredible country to visit with gorgeous landscapes, the architecture and a wonderful culture that provides an out-of-this-world experience. If you want to find out why the streets of Prague are always flooded with tourists, then you will have to acquire a visa to allow yourself into this magical land. Keep reading to know more about acquiring a Czech Republic travel visa.

Types of Czech Republic Visa


Note: All of the visas for the Czech Republic fall under Schengen Visa. Hence, you will have to file the Schengen visa application, but make sure you fill in the right type.

Who Needs a Visa for the Czech Republic?

Indian passport holders travelling to the Czech Republic will need to acquire a Czech Republic visitor visa to travel to the country. They have to obtain a Schengen visa, as the country falls under the Schengen regions and allows the visitor to travel to all the Schengen countries. Although European nationals are exempted from requiring a visa, Indian nationals require it.

Requirement and Validity for Czech Republic Visa for Indians

Before beginning your process of applying for a visa to the Czech Republic, there are certain requirements to keep in mind to ensure that you have everything you need and are sufficiently prepared for the process.

  • An Indian passport with a minimum of 3-6 months of validity from the date of the trip. Also ensure that there are at least two blank pages left. 
  • A fully and accurately completed visa application.
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs satisfactory to the Schengen specifications: 35 mm wide by 45 mm high, 400 dpi at least, plain white background, colour photograph, face should be completely visible, neutral expression and no glasses.
  • Proof of accommodation, flight tickets, financial proof supporting the trip and an itinerary. 
  • Cover letter stating purpose of visit.

Validity

  • Tourist visa: Valid for 90 days within a 180-day period.
  • Business visa: Valid for 90 days within a 180-day period.
  • Friends and family visit visa: Valid for 90 days within a 180-day period.
  • Work visa: Depends on the employment contract. 
  • Transit visa: Valid for 5 days.
  • Schengen visa: Valid for 90 days within a 180-day period.

Application Process for Czech Republic Visitor Visa

While applying for a visa for the Czech Republic, you can either avail an e-visa or apply through an embassy or consulate. Here is a step-by-step guide for both an e-visa and an offline visa for a Czech Republic visa:

Online

Step 1: Visit the official immigration website for the Schengen visa.

Step 2:  Register and create an account in the application portal.

Step 3: Fill out your application form completely and provide accurate information. Upload the required documents, such as the passport, photographs, and travel itinerary.

Step 4: Pay the application fee and submit the application. Simultaneously, schedule an appointment at the nearest VFS Global Centre or any other embassy for your biometric and verification process.

Step 5: You can track your application through the provided application number. Once approved, you will have to collect your passport along with the visa at the nearest embassy.

Offline

Step 1: Visit the nearest VFS Global Centre and collect the application form for a Schengen visa.

Step 2: Complete the application form accurately and fill in all the required details. Also, gather all the required documents, including the passport, travel insurance, financial proof, and cover letter, and get copies of each of them.

Step 3: Visit the VFS Global Centre again and pay the processing fee. Submit the completed application form along with the documents.

Step 4: You will receive the confirmation receipt and a tracking number.

Step 5: You can track your application status with the tracking number. You will receive the approval through mail or SMS. You can either visit the embassy and collect your visa and passport or opt for the courier service.

Czech Republic Visa Processing Time and Fee

The processing fee for a tourist visa, Schengen visa, business visa, family/friends visa and transit visa costs Rs. 8,000 and takes about 10-15 working days to get processed. Whereas a work visa takes around 1-3 months to get processed.

Tips to Guarantee a Successful Czech Republic Visa

The chances of acquiring a Czech Republic travel visa are moderate. So it is important to cover all the bases and ensure you get a successful visa. Here are some helpful tips:

  • Submit complete and accurate documentation.
  • Apply at least 3-4 weeks before your travel date.
  • Ensure your travel insurance meets the Schengen requirements.
  • Provide a detailed itinerary and financial proof.

Common Reasons for Rejection

The acceptance rate for a Czech Republic visa is 85-90%. Here are some common reasons for the visa getting rejected for you to avoid so you’re not part of the 10-15% of rejected applicants:

  • Insufficient financial proof.
  • Incomplete or incorrect documentation.
  • Invalid travel insurance.
  • Suspicious travel intent.

Frequently Asked Questions
1. Is the Czech Republic visa free for Indians?

No, the Czech Republic is not visa-free for Indian citizens. Indian nationals need to apply for a Schengen visa to visit the Czech Republic, as it is part of the Schengen Area.

2. Is a Czech Republic visa easy to get?

The ease of obtaining a Czech Republic visa depends on the completeness and accuracy of your application, as well as your supporting documents. As long as you meet the requirements and provide all necessary documentation, obtaining a Czech visa is relatively straightforward.

3. Is Czech a Schengen visa?

Yes, the Czech Republic is part of the Schengen Area. This means that a Schengen visa allows you to visit the Czech Republic and other Schengen countries during your stay.

4. How much is the Czech Republic visa fee?

The visa fee for a Schengen visa, including the Czech Republic, is approximately €80 (around ₹7,000) for adults. Children between the ages of 6 and 12 pay a reduced fee of €40 (around ₹3,500), while children under 6 are exempt from the fee.

5. How much bank balance is required for a Czech visa?

You are generally required to show proof of financial means to cover at least €55 (around ₹5,000) per day of your stay in the Czech Republic. The total bank balance required depends on the duration of your trip.

6. What is the rejection rate for a Czech visa?

The rejection rate for Czech Republic Schengen visas varies yearly. In recent years, it has been around 5-10%. To improve your chances of approval, ensure your application is complete and accurate, with all required documents submitted.

7. What is the minimum bank balance for a Schengen visa?

The minimum bank balance required for a Schengen visa is typically calculated based on your daily expenses in the destination country. For the Czech Republic, this is approximately €55 per day, so ensure your bank statements reflect sufficient funds for the duration of your trip.

8. How long does a Czech visa take?

The processing time for a Czech Republic visa is typically 15 calendar days from the date of submission. However, during peak seasons or if additional documentation is required, it may take up to 30 or 60 days. It is advisable to apply well in advance of your planned travel dates.
