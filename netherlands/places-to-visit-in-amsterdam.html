Places to visit in Amsterdam

Pick your next Hassle-free holiday with us

Amsterdam is a marvel of engineering, and it still reeks of the ambition, cultural tolerance, and business that propelled the Netherlands to the top of the globe in trade, naval power, culture, and economics during the 17th century Golden Age. It’s also the home of Rembrandt, Anne Frank, the Red Light District, Johan Cruyff, and the Dutch East and West India Companies. Let’s explore the 15 Places to Visit in Amsterdam!

15 Best Places to Visit in Amsterdam

  1. Explore the Canal Cruise
  2. Visit Rijksmuseum
  3. Explore Van Gogh Museum
  4. The Red Light District
  5. Try the Heineken Experience
  6. Enjoy the Efteling Theme Park
  7. Stroll the Keukenhof
  8. Waterlooplein
  9. Visit Anne Frank House
  10. Walking at Vondelpark
  11. Visit the Royal Palace
  12. Rembrandt House Museum

  14. Visit the Albert cuyp Market
  15. Enjoy the Ice Bar

1. Explore the Canal Cruise

The Dutch capital is unquestionably beautiful, but a canal ride allows you to experience it from a new perspective. You’ll witness the lovely facade of majestic canal mansions sliding past you on your Amsterdam canal cruise, and look! There’s the Anne Frank House and the Westerkerk! And did you notice the lovely houseboats? The canals of Amsterdam are a must-see.

The canal ring in Amsterdam is so beautiful. UNESCO has designated it as a tourist attraction. Now is the time to book your Amsterdam canal trip and let the city charm you! Canal cruises are accessible in Amsterdam for both the youth and the elderly; in fact, anybody may enjoy a canal trip in this wonderful city.

2. Visit Rijksmuseum

The reputation of the masterpieces in the Rijksmuseum’s collection is what makes it one of the world’s most famous museums. However, there are many additional pieces in the collection that may surprise you and grab your imagination. Girl in a White Kimono by Breitner and the Asian Pavilion, for instance. The Rijksmuseum will teach you something new no matter how old you are.

The Night Watch is being researched and treated at the Rijksmuseum using cutting-edge technologies. We chose to restore Rembrandt’s masterpiece right on site because it is the most prominent picture in the Gallery of Honour. Not behind the closed doors but in full view of the public in the Night Watch exhibit. Visitors can observe the researchers in action in real-time in the museum or on the internet. It’s known as “Operation Night Watch.”

Explore Van Gogh Museum

The Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam is one the most visited institutions in the world, drawing visitors from all over the globe. In addition, it is owing in part to the fact that it houses the world’s best collection of Vincent van Gogh’s oeuvre, which includes more than 200 paintings, 500 drawings, and 700 letters. The Van Gogh Museum first opened on Museumplein in 1973. It has been enlarged and modernized, ensuring it is a cutting-edge exhibition and tourist space.

A visit to the Van Gogh Museum is a one-of-a-kind and fascinating experience for locals and visitors alike. In addition to Van Gogh’s well-known post-impressionist works, such as his landscapes, self-portraits, and still lifes – sunflowers- the museum also allows visitors to learn about the artist’s growth and compare his works to those of other 19th-century artists.

The Red Light District

The tour allows you to visit Amsterdam’s Red Light District at night when it is at its most vibrant. View a mix of art galleries, coffee shops, and red-lit windows as you stroll through the cobblestone lanes of De Wallen. The guide will assist you in learning more about the area’s history. This walking tour will introduce you to Amsterdam’s bustling nightlife and intriguing reputation.

Men used to come behind the chink with a burning red light in pursuit of women, which is how it earned its name. The Oude Kerk (Old Church), one of Amsterdam’s oldest churches, is one of the city’s main attractions. Even today, red lights are used in the Amsterdam Red Light District., and that’s why evening is the best time to visit.

Try the Heineken Experience

The Heineken Experience is one of the fabulous 15 Places to Visit in Amsterdam in May if you enjoy beer and want to learn more about its history and preparation. As a result, the entire visit will leave you with a lasting impression.

Heineken is, as you may know, one of the most well-known beer brands in the world. The brand’s brewery was established in Amsterdam in the 18th century. As a result, the location offers packages that you can test out based on your time and money.

Enjoy the Efteling Theme Park

The Efteling Theme Park in Amsterdam is the Netherlands’ largest theme park. This is one of the good 15 places to visit in Amsterdam in May. It is a fascinating world filled with engaging stories and exhilarating rides. This is a magical place where you may spend quality time with your family, especially children. This facility is a hit with kids since it brings them closer to their favourite TV characters in a fun and exciting way. Efteling Park is a site where children and adults alike can take on a voyage with Jokie and Jet during the Carnaval Festival, and daredevils can go through the gold mine of Baron 1898 at speeds of up to 90 km/h. You can also glide through Symbolica’s secret hallways and mystical places. Moreover, this is an unstoppable event during your visit, with shimmering lights, chilly entertainment, and warm bonfires.

This Efteling Park Ticket allows you to enter the park without having to wait in line. There’s no need to stand in huge lines; skip the line and spend more time in the park. A visit to this location will undoubtedly be remembered.

Stroll the Keukenhof

Keukenhof, often known as the Garden of Europe, is a must-see on any vacation to the Netherlands. This is one of the world’s best flower gardens, with stunning tulip plantations and fabulous flower gardens. This year, there are over 7 million tulip flowers, representing over 800 different types! Take a journey to this location!

This package includes a Keukenhof entrance ticket as well as round-trip bus transportation from Amsterdam. It’s in Lisse, which is only about an hour’s drive from Amsterdam. From Amsterdam Leidseplein, Amsterdam RAI, Schiphol, Haarlem, Hoofddorp, or Leiden, take advantage of convenient bus transfers! Take a stroll through 15 kilometres of walkways and over 32 acres of lovely flower beds. Seven million flowers bloom, creating a stunning colourful carpet on the ground. Bring your best camera because the area offers some spectacular scenic views that are well worth photographing.

Waterlooplein

The Waterlooplein flea market is the ideal destination in Amsterdam for bargain hunters.  Antiques, books, gems, food & beverages, and bicycles are just a few examples of what you can find. As a result, it could be on your list of things to do in Amsterdam in May.

You may get to the market via numerous forms of transportation, including strolling, riding on the canal, or taking the train.

Visit Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Amsterdam in May. Anne Frank was a Jewish girl who lived in the Netherlands as a child. She kept a diary during WWII, detailing the challenges that Jews endured under the fascist Nazi administration. The structure, which is now almost like a museum, is where she and her family hid from the Nazis.

Moreover, the museum and house are integrated in a way because each step builds upon the previous. Educationally, the museum portion is informative for all ages and backgrounds. Book your tickets in advance.

Walking at Vondelpark

This place must be at the top of your list to wander. The park is a renowned tourist destination with over 45 hectares of land which is a visual joy. Vondelpark has numerous cafes and eateries where you can spend the entire day.

Secondly, this is one of the amazing 15 Places to Visit in Amsterdam in May for a Family Picnic or Solo Adventure. Cycling, skating, and painting are just a few of the activities available in this park.  You can simply relax in a hammock next to the lake. In an open-air theatre, you can sit and see the performances and concerts.

Visit the Royal Palace

The Royal Palace of Amsterdam is one of the must-see attractions in Amsterdam in May, and it is on everyone’s bucket list. The state now presides and Dutch royal occasions are performed. Also, this palace was previously used as a town hall.

Furthermore, the elaborate construction of this palace will astound you, particularly the citizen’s hall, which is one of the structure’s highlights. Moreover, this is home to the Dutch royalties, Pieter van Vollenhoven and Princess Margriet, and it is open to the public all year round.

Rembrandt House Museum

In May, one of the most popular sites to visit in Amsterdam is the Rembrandt House Museum. If you enjoy art and history, you will be blown away by this location. Some of the museum’s objects are by Rembrandt, a well-known Dutch painter.

The museum has also hosted several other works by well-known painters. Furthermore, tourists have praised the museum’s decor and interior, which gives it a traditional appearance. A tour guide is offered for roughly 150 minutes while visiting the museum.

Explore the Artis Zoo

A visit to the Artis zoo is one of the most suitable 15 Places to visit in Amsterdam in May for animal lovers and families with children. You’ll be pleased to learn that the zoo is one of Europe’s oldest. You may see a variety of creatures here, including lions, zebras, elephants, and more. The zoo also contains Micropia and a planetarium, in addition to the animals.

From the zoo’s environmental conservation programs, Jaguars, Polynesian Tree Snails, African Penguins, Asian Elephants, Griffon Vultures, Red Pandas, and other animals are being saved. As a result, it is a fantastic location for wildlife enthusiasts.

Visit the Albert cuyp Market

A visit to the Albert Cuyp market is a must if you wish to learn about this country’s rich food culture and shopping habits, as well as those of Europe. In May, shopping is one of the most popular activities to do in Amsterdam.

Try your bargaining skills at one of the many shops and stalls here. The abundance of fresh food, souvenirs, apparel, and accessories available at the various stalls in this market will dazzle you. And also it was too lively in nature.

Enjoy the Ice bar

The ice bar in Amsterdam is truly a one-of-a-kind venue. Imagine yourself in a room where everything is made of ice, and the temperature is maintained at – 10 degrees Celsius at all times. Of course, it’s for you and it is one of the 15 Places to Visit in Amsterdam in May.

The Amsterdam Ice Bar is a great place to meet up with friends for a drink or two and enjoy a fun night out.
